You already have software for the call — dispatch, incident reports, NERIS. B4C is the other half, and it does two things no records platform does.

B4C only

Tap the truck, not a list

Photo-based apparatus checks on a picture of your actual rig, with a full record of who checked what and when. No rubber-stamping a checklist.

Everyone else: a digital list or form. Station Boss and the enterprise RMS platforms all do apparatus checks as checklists tied to records — not a photo of the truck.

The software writes it. A person signs it.

AI drafts your minutes, drill plans, and recruitment outreach; a qualified human approves before anything's official.

Everyone else: you still do the writing. Their automation is field validation and expiry alerts — at most report-entry assistance, not drafted-and-approved governance.

What B4C leaves to your records system — on purpose

Dispatch, incident reports, NERIS, scheduling, and ePCR are the job of your RMS, and B4C doesn't try to replace it. NFIRS retired January 2026 and NERIS is mandatory, so keep whatever you use for that. B4C sits alongside it as the readiness and accountability half — the part that platform doesn't do. If a chief already runs Station Boss, that's fine: B4C isn't the thing they replace, it's the thing they add.

The one-line version

"You've got the call covered. B4C handles everything before it — the truck checks your crew actually does, and the minutes and outreach that write themselves with a person signing off. Nobody else does those two." If it turns into a feature-count argument, steer back to the two wins at the top.

Verify before you send this

  • Station Boss — AI drafting: marked "not marketed." Their automation is field validation and expiry alerts, not generative drafting — but that's absence of evidence, not a stated "no." Confirm before printing it.
  • Station Boss — governance / minutes / voting: not found across their site. Same caveat — confirm before you claim it.
  • B4C pricing: still the draft $1,500–3,000/yr. Lock the real numbers first.
  • Enterprise RMS column groups ESO, ImageTrend, and First Due. First Due markets "AI-powered documentation," but as report-entry help — not human-signed minutes/outreach. Don't let a prospect equate the two.
